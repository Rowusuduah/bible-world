## SCRIPTURE ANALYSIS

**Psalm 51** — David's prayer after his greatest failure. "Create in me a clean heart, O God." This is the ultimate prompt: a broken input requesting total transformation. Not improvement — replacement. The Psalm moves from confession (intake) to cleansing (analysis) to renewal (generation) to restoration (output). This IS a prompt chain: broken input passes through sequential transformation stages and emerges as something entirely new.

**John 6:1-15** — Feeding the 5,000. Five loaves, two fish, 5,000 fed, 12 baskets left over. The multiplication ratio is staggering: small input, massive output. This is the GrantPilot economic model. A 15-minute intake form (5 loaves) produces a 20-page proposal (5,000 fed). The efficiency of transformation is the entire value proposition.

**John 6:35** — "I am the bread of life." The product IS the sustenance. Not a supplement, not a helper tool. The bread itself. GrantPilot should not be a "writing assistant" — it should BE the proposal. The customer does not want help writing; they want the proposal written.
