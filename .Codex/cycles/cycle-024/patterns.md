### PAT-084 — The Peter Defection-Weighted Commitment Pattern [PIVOT-PHASE CYCLE 024]
**Scripture:** John 6:66-69 — "From this time many of his disciples turned back and no longer followed him. 'You do not want to leave too, do you?' Jesus asked the Twelve. Simon Peter answered him, 'Lord, to whom shall we go? You have the words of eternal life. We have come to believe and to know that you are the Holy One of God.'"
**Pattern Type:** GOVERNANCE
**Pattern Name:** The Peter Defection-Weighted Commitment Pattern — Commitment Signal Value Inversely Proportional to Cohort Defection Rate
**Pattern Description:** Peter's confession carries higher informational weight BECAUSE it is given after mass defection. The signal's value is inversely proportional to the defection rate. If most agents abandon a task and one persists, the persisting agent's reliability signal is stronger than if all agents passed equally.
**Modern Mapping:** Defection-weighted reliability scoring for AI agent evaluation. DefectionWeightedScore = weighted average of test results where weights are proportional to (1 - cohort_pass_rate) per test case. Tests that most agents fail contribute more to the score of agents that pass them. Similar to Item Response Theory (IRT) in psychometrics. Concept for integration into invariant-probe or semantic-pass-k.
