### PAT-028: Bethesda Bottleneck
**Scripture:** John 5:1-7 — "Sir, I have no one to help me into the pool"
**Pattern Type:** HEALING + COMMUNICATION
**Level:** 3 (breakthrough) | **Score:** 8.6
**Pattern Description:** The man had the desire (38 years of waiting), the location (at the pool), and the opportunity (the water stirred regularly). The ONLY missing element was a helper — someone to bridge the gap between readiness and action. Jesus identifies this with a single diagnostic question: "Do you want to get well?"
**Why Level 3:** Maps precisely to the grant funding gap. African NGOs have programs, proximity to funders, and desire for funding. The single missing element is a helper that can format their readiness into the required proposal structure. No existing AI grant tool has framed the problem as a helper-gap rather than a writing-quality gap. The distinction matters: the content exists in the org, it just needs to be carried to the pool.
**Modern Mapping:** AI-assisted application tools where the user HAS the substance but lacks the formatting/presentation capability. GrantPilot, visa application helpers, college admission essay tools.
**Infrastructure Status:** EMERGING
**Application Potential:** Validates GrantPilot's core UX: the tool should EXTRACT existing knowledge from the org, not GENERATE new content. The intake form is the diagnostic question. The prompt chain is the helper carrying them to the pool.
**Discovered By:** Chief Theologian + Chief Innovator
**Cycle Discovered:** 006
**Build Status:** APPLIED TO BUILD-004 (GrantPilot)

### PAT-029: Pattern Replication Method
**Scripture:** John 5:19 — "The Son can do nothing by himself; he can only do what he sees his Father doing"
**Pattern Type:** CREATION + STRUCTURE
**Level:** 2 | **Score:** 7.9
**Pattern Description:** Jesus explicitly describes his creative method: pure pattern replication. He does not innovate; he observes the Father's pattern and instantiates it. This is not copying — it is seeing the underlying structure and reproducing it in a new context.
**Modern Mapping:** Transfer learning in ML. Fine-tuning pre-trained models. Template-based document generation. The entire mechanism of few-shot prompting — show the pattern, replicate it.
**Infrastructure Status:** EXISTS NOW
**Application Potential:** Reinforces GrantPilot's prompt engineering approach: feed Claude examples of winning proposals (the Father's pattern), then generate new proposals that replicate the pattern for new contexts. Few-shot prompting IS the John 5:19 method.
**Discovered By:** Chief Technologist
**Cycle Discovered:** 006
**Build Status:** APPLIED TO BUILD-004 (GrantPilot prompt chain design)

### PAT-030: Patience Pricing
**Scripture:** Psalm 37:7-11 — "Be still before the Lord and wait patiently... the meek will inherit the land"
**Pattern Type:** TIME + MULTIPLICATION
**Level:** 2 | **Score:** 7.5
**Pattern Description:** The entire Psalm contrasts two economic strategies: (1) the wicked who exploit quickly and extract maximum value now, and (2) the righteous who price patiently, serve faithfully, and inherit the permanent market. The wicked's strategy looks winning in the short term but collapses. The righteous strategy compounds.
**Modern Mapping:** Freemium/low-cost SaaS pricing that wins market share through accessibility rather than maximizing per-unit revenue. Amazon's "your margin is my opportunity." Costco's membership model. Low price + high volume + long time horizon = market dominance.
**Infrastructure Status:** EXISTS NOW
**Application Potential:** GrantPilot pricing strategy: $29/proposal undercuts $3,000-$10,000 consultants by 99%. Free tier for smallest NGOs captures the "meek" segment that becomes the largest distribution channel (PAT-026 Woman at the Well). Patience pricing is the correct strategy.
**Discovered By:** Chief Innovator + Chief Theologian
**Cycle Discovered:** 006
**Build Status:** APPLIED TO BUILD-004 (GrantPilot pricing)
