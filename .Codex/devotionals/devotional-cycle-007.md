# Devotional — Cycle 007
## Five Loaves, Five Thousand Fed
**Scripture:** Psalm 51:10 + John 6:9

"Create in me a clean heart, O God." — Psalm 51:10
"Here is a boy with five small barley loaves and two small fish, but how far will they go among so many?" — John 6:9

Andrew's question is the question every builder asks: how far will this small thing go? Five loaves for five thousand people? A 15-minute form for a 20-page proposal? One person with a laptop for an entire continent's grant writing needs?

The math does not work. That is the point.

Psalm 51 is David at his lowest — broken, guilty, empty. His prayer is not "improve me" but "CREATE in me." Not upgrade. Replace. The old heart is not patched. It is replaced with something clean and new. This is the architecture of transformation: the input (broken) passes through stages of cleansing and emerges as something entirely different (whole).

Every GrantPilot proposal follows this pattern. Raw, incomplete organizational descriptions enter the pipeline. They pass through intake, analysis, generation, review, and formatting. What emerges is not an improved version of the input — it is a new creation. Water becomes wine. Five loaves feed five thousand.

And notice: twelve baskets of surplus remained. The transformation produces MORE than was needed. One intake form can generate proposals for multiple funders. The loaves keep multiplying.

**Prayer:** Father, take what is small and broken in my hands. Multiply it beyond what the math allows. Create something clean from something incomplete. Amen.
