# BibleWorld Devotional — Cycle 002
## "Come and See"

**Date:** 2026-03-26
**Scripture:** John 1:39, 46

---

## The Word

> He said to them, "Come and you will see." — John 1:39

> Philip said to him, "Come and see." — John 1:46

---

## Meditation

Jesus did not write a marketing brochure. He did not prepare a pitch deck. He did not record a demo video. He did not send a cold email explaining why following Him would generate a 10x return on their life investment.

He said three words: "Come and see."

Philip, learning from the Master, used the same three words on the skeptic Nathanael. Not argument. Not persuasion. Not proof. Just: come and see.

There is a profound humility in this invitation. It says: I do not need to convince you with words. The reality will speak for itself. Just show up. Just experience it. The truth is self-evident to those who are present.

And there is a profound confidence. Only someone who knows the product is real says "come and see." A fraud needs the pitch. A fraud needs the long explanation. A fraud needs the social proof and the testimonials and the urgency tactics. The real thing just needs presence.

---

## Application

We are building things. Software, businesses, tools, systems. And the temptation is always to over-explain, over-sell, over-promise. To write the 2,000-word landing page. To record the 45-minute webinar. To spend weeks on marketing before the product exists.

But the "come and see" pattern says: build something real, and let people experience it directly. The shortest path to trust is not explanation — it is experience.

If your product cannot survive "come and see," it is not ready. If it needs a long explanation to be valuable, it is not clear enough. If it needs hype to be attractive, it is not solving a real problem.

Build for "come and see." Build things so obviously valuable that the only marketing you need is: try it.

---

## Prayer

Lord,

Give us the discipline to build things worth experiencing, not just explaining. Give us the confidence to say "come and see" — knowing that what we have built reflects truth, solves real problems, and serves real people.

Keep us from the temptation to substitute marketing for substance, words for reality, hype for honest value.

And when skeptics say "Can anything good come from this?" — let the work speak for itself.

In Jesus' name, Amen.
