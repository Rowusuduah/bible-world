# Devotional — Cycle 008
## Dwell in the Shelter
**Scripture:** Psalm 91:1 + John 7:37

"He who dwells in the shelter of the Most High will rest in the shadow of the Almighty." — Psalm 91:1
"Let anyone who is thirsty come to me and drink." — John 7:37

Psalm 91 lists eight threats: terror by night, arrows by day, pestilence in darkness, plague at noonday, a thousand falling at your side, ten thousand at your right hand, the lion, the cobra. Eight threats. Zero fights. Every single threat is neutralized not by combat but by position. You are in the shelter. The threats pass around you.

Jesus stands in the temple on the last day of the feast — the biggest crowd, the highest attention — and makes the simplest possible announcement: "If you are thirsty, come to me." No persuasion. No proof. No argument. Just an open invitation for those who already know they are thirsty.

This is how you launch a product. You do not convince people they have a problem. You find people who ALREADY know they are thirsty and you stand where they can see you. African NGOs losing grant after grant already know they are thirsty. Grant consultants overwhelmed with demand already know they are thirsty. You do not sell to them. You announce to them.

And you position yourself in the shelter — the market niche no competitor occupies. Not the crowded general grant writing market with 17 competitors. The Africa-specific position where zero competitors dwell. That is the shelter. Occupy it. Rest in its shadow. Let the arrows of competition fall on those fighting in the open field.

**Prayer:** Lord, show me the shelter you have already prepared. Give me the courage to occupy it and the peace to rest in it while others fight in the open. Amen.
