# Devotional — Cycle 006
## "Do You Want to Get Well?"
**Scripture:** Psalm 37:7 + John 5:6

"Be still before the Lord and wait patiently for him." — Psalm 37:7
"Do you want to get well?" — John 5:6

For 38 years a man lay beside healing water, unable to reach it. Not because the water was far. Not because he lacked desire. He lacked a helper. One person to bridge the gap between where he was and where healing flowed.

Jesus did not ask "Can you get well?" He asked "Do you WANT to?" The question cuts through every excuse and lands on the only thing that matters: will.

There are organizations across Africa right now lying beside pools of funding. Billions of dollars flow through USAID, IFC, Mastercard Foundation, AfDB. The water stirs regularly. Call after call for proposals goes out. And capable organizations with real programs watch the money go to others — not because their programs are worse, but because they lack someone to carry them to the pool.

Psalm 37 teaches patience in the face of this injustice. The wicked (well-funded consultants charging $5,000 per proposal) appear to prosper. But the meek — the small NGO with the real program and the $29 tool — will inherit the land.

Be the helper at the pool. Build the bridge between readiness and resource. And be patient — the land belongs to those who serve faithfully, not those who extract quickly.

**Prayer:** Lord, show me who is lying beside the pool today, waiting for a helper. Give me the patience of Psalm 37 and the directness of John 5. Amen.
