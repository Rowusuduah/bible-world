*"When Adam had lived 130 years, he had a son in his own likeness, in his own image; and he named him Seth."* (Gen 5:3)

*"Altogether, Adam lived a total of 930 years, and then he died."* (Gen 5:5)

## NUMBERS 23:19 — THE CONSISTENCY BENCHMARK

*"God is not human, that he should lie, or a son of man, that he should change his mind. Does he speak and then not act? Does he promise and then not fulfill?"* (Num 23:19)

Context: Balaam's second oracle at Zophim. Balak brought Balaam to curse Israel; Balaam could only speak what God put in his mouth. Balaam speaks of God's nature as the ground of His reliability: He does not lie, does not change His mind, always fulfills His declarations. The reliability is total, verifiable by output inspection: "Does he speak and then not act?" — the test is behavioral, not claimed.

## PROVERBS 11:1 — THE MEASUREMENT STANDARD

*"The LORD detests dishonest scales, but accurate weights find favor with him."* (Prov 11:1)

Also Proverbs 20:10: *"Differing weights and differing measures — the LORD detests them both."*

Context: Commercial measurement fraud. Merchants used two sets of weights — heavier when buying, lighter when selling. The same scale, the same interaction pattern, but inconsistent measurement. The LORD's standard: one weight, always the same, regardless of context.
