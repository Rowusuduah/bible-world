## CHAPTER 1: PSALM 2

### Full Text (ESV)

**1** Why do the nations rage
    and the peoples plot in vain?
**2** The kings of the earth set themselves,
    and the rulers take counsel together,
    against the LORD and against his Anointed, saying,
**3** "Let us burst their bonds apart
    and cast away their cords from us."

**4** He who sits in the heavens laughs;
    the Lord holds them in derision.
**5** Then he will speak to them in his wrath,
    and terrify them in his fury, saying,
**6** "As for me, I have set my King
    on Zion, my holy hill."

**7** I will tell of the decree:
The LORD said to me, "You are my Son;
    today I have begotten you.
**8** Ask of me, and I will make the nations your heritage,
    and the ends of the earth your possession.
**9** You shall break them with a rod of iron
    and dash them in pieces like a potter's vessel."

**10** Now therefore, O kings, be wise;
    be warned, O rulers of the earth.
**11** Serve the LORD with fear,
    and rejoice with trembling.
**12** Kiss the Son,
    lest he be angry, and you perish in the way,
    for his wrath is quickly kindled.
Blessed are all who take refuge in him.

### Key Observations:
- **Governance structure:** Nations/kings (lower authority) rebel against the LORD and His Anointed (supreme authority). The hierarchy is non-negotiable.
- **Decree pattern (v.7):** The decree is the mechanism of authority. The Son's authority derives from a declared decree, not from conquest. Authority is protocol-based.
- **Heritage distribution (v.8):** "Ask of me, and I will make the nations your heritage." Inheritance is request-based, not seizure-based. The pattern is ask-receive, not take.
- **Rod of iron (v.9):** Enforcement mechanism. Authority without enforcement is meaningless. The rod is the enforcement layer.
- **Refuge model (v.12):** "Blessed are all who take refuge in him." Safety comes from alignment with the authority structure, not from independent power.

## CHAPTER 2: JOHN 1:19-51

### Full Text (ESV)

**19** And this is the testimony of John, when the Jews sent priests and Levites from Jerusalem to ask him, "Who are you?" **20** He confessed, and did not deny, but confessed, "I am not the Christ." **21** And they asked him, "What then? Are you Elijah?" He said, "I am not." "Are you the Prophet?" And he answered, "No." **22** So they said to him, "Who are you? We need to give an answer to those who sent us. What do you say about yourself?" **23** He said, "I am the voice of one crying out in the wilderness, 'Make straight the way of the Lord,' as the prophet Isaiah said."

**24** (Now they had been sent from the Pharisees.) **25** They asked him, "Then why are you baptizing, if you are neither the Christ, nor Elijah, nor the Prophet?" **26** John answered them, "I baptize with water, but among you stands one you do not know, **27** even he who comes after me, the strap of whose sandal I am not worthy to untie." **28** These things took place in Bethany across the Jordan, where John was baptizing.

**29** The next day he saw Jesus coming toward him, and said, "Behold, the Lamb of God, who takes away the sin of the world! **30** This is he of whom I said, 'After me comes a man who ranks before me, because he was before me.' **31** I myself did not know him, but for this purpose I came baptizing with water, that he might be revealed to Israel." **32** And John bore witness: "I saw the Spirit descend from heaven like a dove, and it remained on him. **33** I myself did not know him, but he who sent me to baptize with water said to me, 'He on whom you see the Spirit descend and remain, this is he who baptizes with the Holy Spirit.' **34** And I have seen and have borne witness that this is the Son of God."

**35** The next day again John was standing with two of his disciples, **36** and he looked at Jesus as he walked by and said, "Behold, the Lamb of God!" **37** The two disciples heard him say this, and they followed Jesus. **38** Jesus turned and saw them following and said to them, "What are you seeking?" And they said to him, "Rabbi" (which means Teacher), "where are you staying?" **39** He said to them, "Come and you will see." So they came and saw where he was staying, and they stayed with him that day, for it was about the tenth hour. **40** One of the two who heard John speak and followed Jesus was Andrew, Simon Peter's brother. **41** He first found his own brother Simon and said to him, "We have found the Messiah" (which means Christ). **42** He brought him to Jesus. Jesus looked at him and said, "You are Simon the son of John. You shall be called Cephas" (which means Peter).

**43** The next day Jesus decided to go to Galilee. He found Philip and said to him, "Follow me." **44** Now Philip was from Bethsaida, the city of Andrew and Peter. **45** Philip found Nathanael and said to him, "We have found him of whom Moses in the Law and also the prophets wrote, Jesus of Nazareth, the son of Joseph." **46** Nathanael said to him, "Can anything good come out of Nazareth?" Philip said to him, "Come and see." **47** Jesus saw Nathanael coming toward him and said of him, "Behold, an Israelite indeed, in whom there is no deceit!" **48** Nathanael said to him, "How do you know me?" Jesus answered him, "Before Philip called you, when you were under the fig tree, I saw you." **49** Nathanael answered him, "Rabbi, you are the Son of God! You are the King of Israel!" **50** Jesus answered him, "Because I said to you, 'I saw you under the fig tree,' do you believe? You will see greater things than these." **51** And he said to him, "Truly, truly, I say to you, you will see heaven opened, and the angels of God ascending and descending on the Son of Man."

### Key Observations:
- **Identity verification protocol (v.19-23):** The priests interrogate John with a structured identity query. John authenticates by negation first ("I am not the Christ"), then by declaration ("I am the voice"). This is an authentication pattern: verify by eliminating false claims before making the positive assertion.
- **Referral chain (v.35-42):** John refers disciples to Jesus. Andrew finds Peter. Philip finds Nathanael. This is a multi-level referral network: the first trusted node (John) generates second-order nodes (Andrew, another disciple), who generate third-order nodes (Peter), and so on. Each referral is trust-weighted.
- **"Come and see" protocol (v.39, v.46):** Both Jesus and Philip use "come and see" as the conversion mechanism. Not "here is my pitch." Not "let me explain." The mechanism is direct experience. The product demos itself.
- **Renaming as re-identification (v.42):** Jesus renames Simon to Cephas/Peter (Rock). A new identity is assigned based on future function, not past history. The name is a schema declaration of future role.
- **Pre-knowledge as trust signal (v.47-49):** Jesus demonstrates knowledge of Nathanael before meeting him. This creates instant trust. Nathanael's skepticism ("Can anything good come from Nazareth?") is overcome not by argument but by demonstrated knowledge. Personalization collapses sales cycles.
